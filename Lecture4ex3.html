Bindle and the Iron Mines

Bindle Spinepuller looked at the cold hat in her hands and felt cross.

She walked over to the window and reflected on her stark surroundings. She had always loved deserted Pits of Moraria with its inexpensive, inquisitive iron ingots. It was a place that encouraged her tendency to feel cross.

Then she saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the figure of Katy Noris. Katy was a delightful gamer with wobbly lips and charming warts.

Bindle gulped. She glanced at her own reflection. She was a sinister, snooty, whiskey drinker with short lips and skinny warts. Her friends saw her as a jolly, joyous juggler. Once, she had even jumped into a river and saved a cruel comatose mage.

But not even a sinister person who had once jumped into a river and saved a cruel comatose mage, was prepared for what Katy had in store today.

The snow flurried like walking foxes, making Bindle worried.

As Bindle stepped outside and Katy came closer, she could see the resonant glint in his eye.

"Look Bindle," growled Katy, with a cold-blooded glare that reminded Bindle of delightful owls. "I hate you and I want revenge. You owe me 9908 gold coin."

Bindle looked back, even more worried and still fingering the cold hat. "Katy, he is my iron golem," she replied.

They looked at each other with concerned feelings, like two melted, mammoth mice jumping at a very splendid bar mitzvah, which had acid jazz music playing in the background and two admirable uncles rampaging to the beat.

Bindle studied Katy's wobbly lips and charming warts. Eventually, she took a deep breath. "I'm afraid I declared myself bankrupt," explained Bindle. "You will never get your money."

"No!" objected Katy. "You lie!"

"I do not!" retorted Bindle. "Now get your wobbly lips out of here before I hit you with this cold hat."

Katy looked jumpy, his wallet raw like a kindly, kaleidoscopic kettle.

Bindle could actually hear Katy's wallet shatter into 9908 pieces. Then the delightful gamer hurried away into the distance.

Not even a glass of whiskey would calm Bindle's nerves tonight.
